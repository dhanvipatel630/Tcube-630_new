The Gateway of India, located in Mumbai, Maharashtra, is a historic monument built in 1924 during the British rule. Overlooking the Arabian Sea, it is one of the city’s most famous landmarks.

Marine Drive, also known as the Queen’s Necklace, is a 3.6 km long boulevard in South Mumbai, along the Arabian Sea. It is famous for its beautiful sea-facing promenade, where people come to relax, walk, or enjoy the sunset.

Siddhivinayak Temple, located in Prabhadevi, Mumbai, is one of the most famous and revered temples dedicated to Lord Ganesha.The temple is especially popular during Ganesh Chaturthi and is considered a spiritual landmark of Mumbai.

Juhu Beach, located in Mumbai, is one of the most popular and famous beaches in India. It is well known for its beautiful sunset views, street food like bhel puri and pav bhaji, and as a favorite spot for both tourists and locals.

Shaniwar Wada is a historic fortification located in Pune, Maharashtra.The fort is known for its grand architecture, massive gates, beautiful fountains, and intricate wooden carvings

The Dagadusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple is one of the most famous temples in Pune, dedicated to Lord Ganesha.

Tamhini Ghat is a beautiful mountain pass located near Pune, Maharashtra. It is famous for its lush green valleys, waterfalls, misty hills, and scenic views, especially during the monsoon season. The ghat connects Pune to the Konkan region and is a popular spot for nature lovers, trekking, and photography.

Lonavala is a famous hill station in Maharashtra, located around 65 km from Pune and 80 km from Mumbai. Known for its scenic valleys, waterfalls, lush greenery, and pleasant climate, it is a popular weekend getaway. Major attractions include Bhushi Dam, Tiger’s Leap, Rajmachi Fort, and the nearby Khandala. Lonavala is also well known for its chikki (a sweet snack made of jaggery and nuts).

Trimbakeshwar Temple near Nashik is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. Built in black stone, it is known for its unique linga with three faces representing Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva. The temple is also the source of the holy Godavari River and is an important pilgrimage site.

Ajanta Caves are a group of 30 rock-cut Buddhist caves located near Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar (Aurangabad), Maharashtra. These caves date back to around the 2nd century BCE to the 6th century CE and are among the finest examples of ancient Indian art, architecture, and spirituality.
